Dry sausage: male name,
Delicatessen product based on seasoned minced meat and presented for sale in casings. (A distinction is made between cooked sausages and dry sausages, which undergo ripening and drying.)
Source: larousse.fr

In Latin, salsus means "salty". The Greeks and Romans are believed to be the origin of the sausage and dry sausage. We find sausage recipes dating from the time of the Roman Empire.
In addition, as an accompaniment to cervoise, the Gauls consumed pulses, beans, lentils and above all pigs. Known cured meats from the Gallo-Roman era are salted pork, ham and, in particular, dry sausage.
